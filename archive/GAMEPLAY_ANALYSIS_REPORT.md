# Dice of Dionysus - Comprehensive Gameplay Analysis Report

## Executive Summary

After conducting a thorough analysis of the Dice of Dionysus game mechanics, economy, scoring system, and balance, I've identified several critical issues and areas for improvement. The game shows promise but has significant balance problems that could impact player experience.

## Key Findings

### 🚨 Critical Issues

1. **Economy Imbalance**: Players earn too little gold (only +1 per score) while shop items cost 3-10 gold
2. **Scoring System Problems**: Base scoring values are too low for a satisfying experience
3. **Boon Power Disparity**: Some boons are significantly more powerful than others
4. **Shop Frequency**: Shop visits every 3 turns may be too frequent for the economy

### 📊 Detailed Analysis

## Economy Analysis

### Current Economy Flow
- **Gold Income**: +1 gold per score (13 total per game)
- **Gold Expenses**: 
  - Boons: 3-10 gold each
  - Artifacts: 10 gold each
  - Packs: 4-6 gold each
- **Interest**: +1-5 gold per shop visit (every 3 turns)

### Problems Identified
1. **Income vs Expenses Mismatch**: Players earn ~13 gold but need 20+ gold for meaningful purchases
2. **Interest System**: Too low to compensate for the income gap
3. **Shop Frequency**: Every 3 turns creates pressure to spend without sufficient income

### Recommendations
- Increase gold reward per score to +2 or +3
- Reduce boon costs to 2-6 gold range
- Increase interest cap to 8-10 gold
- Consider shop visits every 4-5 turns instead of 3

## Scoring System Analysis

### Current Scoring Values
- **Upper Section**: 1-6 pips per die (max 30 per category)
- **Lower Section**: Fixed values (25-50 pips)
- **Bonus Categories**: 35 pips for section completion

### Problems Identified
1. **Low Base Values**: Upper section maxes at 30 pips, making it less rewarding
2. **Favour System**: Base 1x favour makes scoring feel underwhelming
3. **Bonus Categories**: 35 pips may not be enough incentive

### Recommendations
- Increase upper section scoring (e.g., 2-12 pips per die)
- Increase lower section base values by 10-20 pips
- Consider higher bonus values (50-100 pips)

## Boon Balance Analysis

### Overpowered Boons
1. **Chaos Primordial** (10 gold): Doubles all favour gains - extremely powerful
2. **Mt Olympus** (6 gold): +1 favour per worship used - scales too well
3. **Prometheus' Gift** (6 gold): +3 favour to all hands - very strong

### Underpowered Boons
1. **Achilles' Heel** (3 gold): +10 pips but -1 gold per roll - too punishing
2. **Lethe Waters** (4 gold): Ignores low dice but only +15 final pips - too weak
3. **Icarus' Wings** (4 gold): +10 pips per unused roll + break chance - too risky

### Balanced Boons
1. **Hestia's Hearth** (3 gold): +3 favour for odd/even - reasonable condition
2. **Charon's Ferry Fare** (3 gold): +1 gold per score - good economy boost
3. **Midas Touch** (4 gold): +5 pips per 10 gold - scales well

### Recommendations
- **Nerf Chaos Primordial**: Change to +50% favour instead of +100%
- **Buff Achilles' Heel**: Reduce gold penalty or increase pip bonus
- **Rework Lethe Waters**: Make it more impactful
- **Adjust Icarus' Wings**: Remove break chance or increase pip bonus

## Worship Card Analysis

### Current System
- All worship cards cost 3 gold
- Each provides +1 favour for specific categories
- Static effects with no scaling

### Problems Identified
1. **No Scaling**: Worship cards don't improve over time
2. **Limited Synergy**: Few interactions between worship cards
3. **Category Locking**: Some worship cards require unlocked categories

### Recommendations
- Add worship level progression system
- Create worship card synergies
- Ensure all worship cards work with base categories

## Artifact Analysis

### Current Artifacts
- All cost 10 gold (very expensive)
- Provide slot increases or shop improvements
- No immediate gameplay impact

### Problems Identified
1. **High Cost**: 10 gold is too expensive for the economy
2. **Limited Impact**: Effects are mostly passive
3. **No Progression**: Artifacts don't upgrade or improve

### Recommendations
- Reduce artifact costs to 6-8 gold
- Add more impactful effects
- Consider artifact upgrade system

## Libation (House Rule) Analysis

### Current Libations
- All cost 3 gold
- Provide die face enhancements
- One-time use effects

### Problems Identified
1. **Limited Impact**: Enhancements may not be noticeable
2. **No Synergy**: Libations don't work together
3. **High Cost**: 3 gold for temporary effects

### Recommendations
- Reduce costs to 2 gold
- Make effects more impactful
- Add libation synergies

## Gameplay Flow Issues

### Turn Structure Problems
1. **Rolling**: 3 rolls per turn is standard but may need adjustment
2. **Scoring**: No penalty for scratching categories
3. **Shop Timing**: Every 3 turns may be too frequent

### Recommendations
- Consider 4 rolls per turn for more strategic depth
- Add small penalty for scratching (e.g., -1 gold)
- Reduce shop frequency to every 4-5 turns

## Technical Issues Found

### Code Quality Issues
1. **Error Handling**: Some areas lack proper error handling
2. **Performance**: Multiple DOM queries could be optimized
3. **Memory**: Some objects may not be properly cleaned up

### UI/UX Issues
1. **Feedback**: Limited visual feedback for actions
2. **Information**: Some game states not clearly communicated
3. **Accessibility**: Could benefit from better accessibility features

## Priority Recommendations

### High Priority (Fix Immediately)
1. **Increase gold income** to +2-3 per score
2. **Reduce boon costs** to 2-6 gold range
3. **Nerf Chaos Primordial** boon
4. **Buff underpowered boons**

### Medium Priority (Next Update)
1. **Improve scoring values** across all categories
2. **Add worship progression system**
3. **Reduce artifact costs**
4. **Improve libation effects**

### Low Priority (Future Updates)
1. **Add more boon variety**
2. **Implement artifact upgrades**
3. **Add libation synergies**
4. **Improve UI feedback**

## Conclusion

Dice of Dionysus has a solid foundation but suffers from significant balance issues that could impact player enjoyment. The economy is the most critical issue, followed by scoring system improvements and boon balance. Addressing these issues will create a more engaging and satisfying gameplay experience.

The game shows promise with its unique theme and mechanics, but requires careful balancing to reach its full potential. Focus on the high-priority recommendations first, then gradually implement the medium and low-priority improvements.
